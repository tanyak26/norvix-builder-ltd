Process

Make the work legible.

A clear process helps every decision feel less rushed: what is being built, what needs protection, what comes first and what finish is expected.

Four stages

Simple enough to follow, detailed enough to trust.

Each job is different, but the rhythm stays consistent so expectations are clear.

Listen

Understand the property, goal, timescale, budget expectations and constraints.

Inspect

Review photos or visit where needed to understand access, materials and condition.

Coordinate

Agree the work scope, likely order, preparation needs and communication route.

Deliver

Carry out the construction work and leave the area ready for use or next stage.

What to prepare

Good details save time on site.

Before contact, collect the basics: address area, photos, rough measurements, access notes, parking, preferred timing and the outcome you want.

  • Photos of the space from several angles.
  • Notes on existing issues such as cracks, damp, damage or awkward access.
  • Any deadline, tenant access or family schedule that affects the work.
